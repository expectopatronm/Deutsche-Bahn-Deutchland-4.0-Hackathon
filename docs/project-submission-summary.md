# TechTrasse Submission Summary

This is a public, privacy-clean summary of the original Deutschland 4.0 submission material for Team TechTrasse.

## Project Title

Nutzung von Kamerasystemen zur optimierten Fahrgastflussregelung

## Team

Team TechTrasse, created by Shankar Kumar and Matthias Eckert.

## Elevator Pitch

Many stations already have camera infrastructure, but that infrastructure is usually most useful after an event has happened. TechTrasse proposes using existing camera systems with machine learning to support safer and faster passenger movement in real time.

The project focuses on boarding and transfer situations. Passenger groups often collect around a few doors instead of distributing themselves along the platform. This can block passengers who need to exit, slow down boarding, and increase the risk of missed connections. TechTrasse detects these patterns and imagines station feedback through announcements, displays, or visual guidance so people are nudged toward better distribution.

## Phase 1

The first phase focuses on S-Bahn settings, where door positions are more predictable and vehicle types are more consistent. Camera images identify door zones, detect passenger groups, estimate entry and exit movement, and trigger audio or visual feedback when groups form in suboptimal places.

## Later Phases

The concept can later expand to long-distance trains, where the system would need to predict likely stopping positions and support passengers before the train arrives. In a broader station-wide version, visual nudging could include floor arrows, waiting zones, exit paths, and door-capacity indicators.

## Problems Addressed

- Suboptimal group formation during transfers
- Door blocking during boarding and alighting
- Missed connections despite timetable buffer
- Limited real-time insight into transfer behavior
- Risk situations near platform edges or in dense crowds
- Manual or delayed detection of incidents

## Why It Is Innovative

The idea reuses existing railway infrastructure and makes it operationally active. Instead of treating cameras only as retrospective security tools, TechTrasse turns visual data into immediate flow guidance for passengers and staff.

## Practical Needs For A Real Pilot

A real deployment would need information about station camera systems, representative test data, privacy and retention rules, speaker/display integration options, and station-specific calibration for door positions and platform geometry.
